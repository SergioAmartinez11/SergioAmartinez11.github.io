My autobiography

Martinez, Sergio A.

Monday, May 23rd 2022

About me

Born in Tijuana on September 11, 1998, I was the second child of my parents Sergio and Gabriela, who had just moved to live in Rosarito. I spend the first five years of my life mostly in my grandparent's restaurant in Puerto nuevo where my parents also worked. At the age of five, I was in Carmen Cerdan's kindergarten where I could play a lot with all the other kids and also had my first English class ever, the first English words that I learned were colors and numbers. Due to my age, I could only be in kindergarten for one year, after that, I went to elementary school for the next six years of my life, making new friends, new experiences, and trying sports for the first time. I was enrolled in Pedro Moreno elementary school as my sister was. There I was able to meet some really good friends and some of them still are. In my fifth grade summer, I tried to play baseball on my best friend's team but eventually quit, to play basketball. Finally, in my sixth grade, I discover what computers can do if you know how to use them and I was amazed by them.

At thirteen I was attending the Amado Nervo middle school and signed to the computer lab class for the next three years of my life. It was a very interesting part of my life because I struggle to make friends but enjoyed learning about all sorts of topics, especially computer science. I start being serious about basketball and trained every day after class with the idea of playing high school basketball and eventually in college. Middle school wasn't the best part of my life but it set what I am now.

After that, I got into high school, Cobach Rosarito. I was first picked in the high school basketball tryouts as a freshman and that was a goal for me. I fit into the team, all my teammates were great guys and good players. Played for three years and the highest we got was winning a state tournament but we didn't pass the nationals. My high school years were the best, I met amazing people and friends, also was a stage of discovering myself in different areas such as personal relations, what I like to do, and what I don't like. Found new hobbies and my extreme love for coffee. My curiosity for programming and computer sciences increased and I give myself the task of learning the basics of software development for a future college career. At this point, I began to explore all the different options for university, and end up finding out that computer engineering at UABC was the best option for me. After passing the test for getting into university I was finally in my dream career and excited for the upcoming.

Where do I see myself in five years?

Professionally

I see myself working as a senior full-stack engineer for an American company like Teradata, Integon, or Amazon with the possibility of migrating to the states or a different country. Also having personal side projects like a coffee shop with my own kind of coffee beans.

Personally

I see myself getting married with my actual relationship, and living on our own. Still hanging out with the same friends, and taking time to spend with my family.

What am I studying?

I'm studying my sixth semester of computer engineering at The Faculty of Chemical Science and Engineering. So far it has been a great experience with ups and downs. The most difficult part of my career for me was designing complex digital circuits.

Computer engineering is a branch of electronic engineering where you have to learn a lot from hardware design, and software design, and how to integrate both to create a functional system. By the end of your bachelor's, you'll have training in software engineering and electronic engineering, making it a very complete and extensive professional career.
